A Brief Few Words

Cigarettes contain about 0 different chemicals, and over 70 are known carcinogens.

Tobacco cigarettes contain chemicals which can have devastating effects to human health. There is no safe limit for smoking. Tar is one the highly damaging components of cigarette smoke. Tar is the solid brown particles suspended in smoke, and contain cancer causing substances.

Tar is also sticky and stains teeth, nails and lung tissue. During the process, carbon monoxide is also produced. Carbon monoxide is an odourless and colourless gas, which will replace the oxygen in the bloodstream. Carbon monoxide can kill in minutes if inhaled in large doses.

Oxidizing chemicals are also found in smoke. These damage heart muscles and blood vessels. Reactions with cholesterol will cause a build up of fatty material on the walls of arteries, increasing blood pressure.

Last year, Australia attributed 0 cases of cancer to tobacco.

Tobacco is the biggest preventable cause of death in Australia. The Australian Burden of Disease study found that one in every seven deaths was caused due to smoking. As soon as you breathe in smoke from tobacco, the symptoms start right away. An everyday smoker will find that their blood is thicker, and more prone to clotting. Their heart rate's and pressure will be significantly higher then none-smokers, which places a lot of pressure on the heart. Arteries also narrow, which decreases oxygen supply to vital organs. Smoking can also lead to fatal diseases such as COPD, pneumonia & various forms of lung cancer.

Let's build a world where preventable deaths aren't a problem.
